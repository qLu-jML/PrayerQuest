Architectural decisions of record (2026-04-16):
  - **Offline-first, sign-in optional.** The entire app — prayer modes, gamification, gratitude, answered prayers, fasting, library, crisis mode, onboarding — works 100% offline with no account of any kind. Prayer Groups and Prayer Partners are the **only** features that require the network, and they are **strictly optional**. A user can install PrayerQuest, use it every day for years, and never sign in. Groups/Partners are gated behind Google Sign-In via Firebase Auth; when signed in, the app pulls/pushes group data to Firestore. Firebase is not touched at all when the user is signed out.
  - Hearts and freezes live on StreakData (not UserStats). Gamification streak-protection reads/writes from StreakData.
  - Prayer Groups use **Google Sign-In** (via Firebase Auth) plus real-time Firestore snapshot listeners with Firestore's offline cache — not manual refresh. The Repository layer owns listener lifecycle (attach on group screen open, detach on disposal). Offline behaviour is handled by Firestore's cache; writes queue automatically. (Note: earlier drafts of this DD called for anonymous Firebase Auth; shipping code uses Google Sign-In because users need a durable identity to move between devices and to be reachably invited into groups. See §3.7.1.)
  - Deprecated-and-deleted prayer modes (do not resurrect without DD change): Scripture Soak, Contemplative Silence, Gratitude Blast. Scripture Soak and Contemplative Silence are captured for a future release in §9.1 and §9.2. Gratitude Blast's rapid-fire behaviour is folded into the Gratitude Section as "Speed Round" — it is no longer a standalone prayer mode.
  - PrayerMode enum values are the 10 MVP modes: GUIDED_ACTS, VOICE_RECORD, PRAYER_JOURNAL, INTERCESSION_DRILL, FLASH_PRAY_SWIPE, DAILY_EXAMEN, LECTIO_DIVINA, BREATH_PRAYER, PRAYER_BEADS, DAILY_OFFICE.

1. VISION & MISSION
- Create the most engaging, effective, and faith-building prayer app ever made — warm enough for a grieving widow, deep enough for a monastic, fun enough for a teenager.
- Combine proven habit-formation science (implementation intentions, gratitude psychology, active engagement) with deep Duolingo/Habitica-style gamification.
- Goal: Help users "pray without ceasing" (1 Thessalonians 5:17), cultivate an intimate walk with God, and build lifelong prayer habits that actually stick — while visibly seeing the fruit of their prayers through answered testimonies, a growing record of God's blessings, and shared prayer support in community.
- Tradition-inclusive: Protestant, Catholic, Orthodox, Anglican, and non-denominational users should all feel the app was designed for them. Traditional modes (Rosary, Daily Office, Lectio Divina, Examen) are opt-in via onboarding but are first-class citizens when enabled.
- Differentiator vs. Pray.com / Echo / Abide / Prayer Notebook: rich multi-sensory prayer modes, powerful reward system for actually praying, seamless voice-to-text, answer-tracking with partial updates, a beautiful unified Prayer Library (famous prayers + names of God), daily Gratitude with photo attachments, invite-only Firebase-backed Prayer Groups for shared intercession, 1-on-1 Prayer Partners, big celebration moments when prayers are answered, and a Year in Review that makes users cry (in a good way).

2. TARGET AUDIENCE
- Christians of all ages and denominations who want consistent prayer but struggle with distraction or dry spells.
- Teens, young adults, busy parents, pastors, small groups, families, and seasoned contemplatives.
- Auditory, kinesthetic, reflective, and competitive learners.
- Users from liturgical traditions who have been under-served by evangelical-flavored prayer apps.

3. FEATURES

3.1 Prayer Engagement Engine — "Many Options & Tricks"
The core of PrayerQuest is a rich, multi-sensory prayer engine that gives users 10 different polished ways to actually pray any topic, list, famous prayer, or group request. Every mode is designed purely for engagement and expression — there is no memorization or spaced-repetition scheduler for prayer items.

Goal: Prevent prayer boredom, support every learning style and tradition, and maximize long-term habit formation through joyful, rewarding prayer action.

All items in this section are [NEEDS IMPLEMENTING] unless otherwise marked.

3.1.1 Prayer Habit & Reward System — The Smart Habit Keeper
- Simple completion-based tracker (no SM-2 or due-review intervals). Every logged prayer session earns XP based on duration, modes used, and items prayed for.
- For each prayer item we persist in UserPrayerProgress (Room entity):
  - Last prayed date
  - Total prayer sessions
  - Total minutes prayed
  - Current status (Active / Partially Answered / Answered)
- After every session users tap a four-button grade row (Again/Hard/Good/Easy) plus optional "How deep was this prayer?" slider that feeds XP and streak logic.
- Daily "Pray Now" queue is user-driven: they choose active lists or famous prayers — no automated "due" pressure.
- Voice-to-text is available in every text or journal mode (SpeechRecognizer with real-time transcription and easy edit).
- Heat-map of most-prayed topics (visual encouragement).

3.1.2 MVP Prayer Modes (10 total — all fully polished for v1.0)

1. **Guided ACTS Walkthrough** (Easiest — Introduction)
   Step-by-step Adoration → Confession → Thanksgiving → Supplication with prompts and timers. Tap-to-advance, optional TTS voice prompts.

2. **Voice Record & Reflect** (Audio-first)
   Full voice-to-text prayer recording (real-time transcription + easy edit). Optional playback of past recordings for reflection. "Skip scoring" escape hatch.

3. **Prayer Journal** (Free-Type)
   Blank screen with date + prompt. Voice-to-text button is prominently featured. On submit: beautiful formatted recap + option to link to specific prayer items.

4. **Intercession Drill** (List-based)
   Tap-bank of people/needs from your active lists. Tap each to pray (30-second timer + quick thanks/confession chips). Voice-to-text option for each item.

5. **Flash-Pray Swipe** (Quick engagement)
   The app generates a short list (default 5–8 items) from the user's active prayers, group items, or famous prayers. Each card shows the prayer topic. User swipes in any direction to dismiss the card and log it as "prayed for." A persistent "Close Prayer" button at the bottom lets the user end the session whenever they want, logging everything swiped so far. Designed to feel effortless: "I have two minutes, let me at least touch each of these before God."

6. **Daily Examen** (Ignatian, Traditional)
   The classic 5-step Ignatian examen: Gratitude → Review the day → Notice emotions → Confess shortcomings → Resolve and renew. Each step has a gentle prompt, optional voice-to-text, and a 60–90 second suggested time. End-of-day positioning in the UI. Logs a Gratitude entry automatically from the first step.

7. **Lectio Divina** (Contemplative, Traditional)
   Four-step monastic reading practice: Lectio (read) → Meditatio (meditate) → Oratio (pray) → Contemplatio (rest). Ships with a curated bundled pack of 50+ prayer-ready Scripture passages (Psalms, Paul's prayers, Beatitudes, Song of Solomon selections, etc.) so the mode works offline and does not require ScriptureQuest. User can also paste their own passage.

8. **Breath Prayer** (Short, Rhythmic, Accessible)
   Paced inhale-exhale animation with a short repeatable prayer (e.g., the Jesus Prayer: "Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God" [inhale] / "have mercy on me, a sinner" [exhale]). Ships with 10+ classical breath prayers. Default 3-minute session. Excellent for streak-saving on busy days and for anxious moments.

9. **Prayer Beads / Rosary** (Traditional)
   Decade tracker with traditional prayers. Visual beads fill as the user taps through. Ships with: the full Rosary (Joyful, Sorrowful, Glorious, Luminous mysteries), the Anglican Prayer Rope (33 beads), the Jesus Prayer rope (Eastern Orthodox, 100-knot default), and a customizable "beads of your own" mode for Protestant users who want to build a custom decade. Haptic tap on each bead.

10. **Daily Office** (Liturgical, Traditional)
    One mode that auto-detects the time of day and surfaces the appropriate hour: Morning Prayer (Lauds), Midday Prayer (Sext), Evening Prayer (Vespers), or Compline (before bed). Each hour has a short structured liturgy: opening versicle, a psalm, a short reading, a collect, and the Lord's Prayer. Ships with a curated ecumenical version drawing from the Book of Common Prayer public-domain text. User can pick their tradition (Anglican / Catholic / "Simple Ecumenical") in onboarding, and the content adapts.

Note: **Gratitude Blast** from the previous draft has been folded into the Gratitude Section (§3.5) as a "Speed Round" option, so there is no longer a standalone Gratitude prayer mode — gratitude logging is a first-class section of the app, not a one-off mode.

Scripture Soak and Contemplative Silence have been moved to [FUTURE UPDATE] — see §9.

3.1.3 Tradition-Aware Mode Picker UI (The "Netflix of Prayer Modes")
Purpose: present 10 modes (plus Library items that can launch modes) without overwhelming users, while making every tradition feel at home.

- **Onboarding question**: "Which prayer traditions are part of your walk?" Options: Protestant / Evangelical, Catholic, Orthodox, Anglican / Episcopal, Non-denominational (all welcome). Multi-select allowed. Choice pre-configures which modes are enabled by default.
- **Settings → Prayer Modes**: every mode has an individual on/off toggle so users can customize freely. A Baptist curious about Lectio Divina can flip it on in 10 seconds; a Catholic who doesn't want to see Flash-Pray can turn it off.
- **Mode Picker screen** uses horizontal shelves (Netflix-style):
  - "Quick (under 3 min)": Flash-Pray Swipe, Breath Prayer
  - "Guided": ACTS Walkthrough, Daily Examen, Lectio Divina
  - "Expressive": Voice Record, Prayer Journal, Intercession Drill
  - "Traditional": Daily Office, Prayer Beads / Rosary
  - Shelves for traditions the user opted out of are collapsed under an "Explore more traditions" footer link — discoverable but out of the way.
- Each mode card shows: icon, mode name, estimated duration, and a "last used" timestamp.

3.1.4 Prayer Session Flow
- "Start Prayer" from Home → Mode Picker → PrayerSessionScreen.
- PrayerSessionViewModel loads user-chosen active lists, famous prayers, or group requests (default 8 items), and runs the selected mode.
- Optional Prayer Preview step before each mode (shows item + voice-to-text ready button).
- Session ends with summary showing XP earned, streak progress, level-up, badges, and encouragement ("Keep seeking Him!", "God hears you!", "He is faithful!").

3.1.5 Technical Implementation Notes
- 100% offline for all solo prayer modes. Reuses ScriptureQuest tech stack and Collection data layer.
- Voice-to-text everywhere via SpeechRecognizer (real-time + editable).
- Prayer lists stored as PrayerCollection + cross-refs.
- Firebase is used for Prayer Groups and Prayer Partners only (§3.7 and §3.8); nothing else requires network connectivity. Signing in is **optional** — only required if the user wants to use Groups/Partners. Everywhere else in the app, signed-in and signed-out users have identical experiences.

3.2 Onboarding Flow — First-Run Full Setup [NEEDS IMPLEMENTING]
Purpose: Get users comfortable fast, while quietly configuring tradition, notifications, and first prayer items. Must feel warm and encouraging, never like a form.

The onboarding is 7 lightweight steps. At any point the user can tap "Skip setup, I'll configure later" and land on Home with sensible defaults.

1. **Welcome & Vision** — short animated screen: "Pray without ceasing. Grow in gratitude. See God's faithfulness." → Continue.
2. **Name & Goal** — user's preferred first name + daily prayer-minutes goal (3/5/10/15/custom) + daily gratitude count (1/3/5).
3. **Tradition Selection** — multi-select chips for Protestant/Evangelical, Catholic, Orthodox, Anglican/Episcopal, Non-denominational. This pre-enables matching modes and Library content.
4. **Liturgical Calendar Awareness** — "Would you like PrayerQuest to follow the Christian calendar?" If yes, user picks Western (Roman/Protestant) or Eastern (Orthodox) calendar. Seasonal prayer packs for Advent, Lent, Holy Week, Easter, and Pentecost auto-surface during those seasons.
5. **Notification Times & Quiet Hours** — user sets up to three daily prayer reminders (morning / midday / evening defaults) with per-time-of-day reminder personalities ("Morning — start the day with Him", "Midday — a quick breath prayer", "Evening — examen and gratitude"). User sets quiet hours (no notifications between X and Y).
6. **First Prayer Collection** — app auto-seeds a "Foundational Prayers" active collection and lets the user add 2–3 people or topics they want to pray for. Voice-to-text button prominent.
7. **First Gratitude Entry** — "Before we go, what's one thing you're thankful for today?" User logs their first gratitude. Celebration animation + first badge earned (Gratitude Starter, day 1/7).

Onboarding completion = first streak day earned, first XP awarded, user lands on Home with quests ready.

3.3 Formation Mode — Per-Item Habit Primer [NEEDS IMPLEMENTING]
(Distinct from the first-run Onboarding above.) When the user adds a new prayer item, Formation Mode is an optional 4–6 step progressive cycle that helps them start praying for it immediately: define it → voice-record a first prayer → set a trigger moment → optional Scripture link → celebrate. 3.4 Gamification System (The Retention Hook) [NEEDS IMPLEMENTING]
- XP & Leveling: purely numerical, uncapped curve. Level label is "Level N" — no named tiers. Curve: threshold(L) = floor(100 × (1.5^(L−1) − 1)); Level 1 starts at 0 XP and each subsequent level requires exponentially more total XP. Same thresholds as ScriptureQuest; the progression is intentionally open-ended so dedicated intercessors keep earning new level-up moments beyond Level 50 / 75 / 100.
- Daily Streaks with dual protection (freezes + hearts) — identical logic.
- Daily Quests — 3 rotating slots (PRAY X MINUTES, USE ≥3 MODES, JOURNAL, LOG GRATITUDE, PRAY FOR GROUP ITEM, COMPLETE EXAMEN, etc.) + full-set bonus.
- Badges & Achievements — 65+ at launch spanning streak tiers (3 / 7 / 30 / 50 / 100 / 365 days), total prayer minutes (1 h / 10 h / 50 h / 100 h), items prayed (1 / 50 / 200 / 500), mode diversity, per-mode mastery (ACTS, Lectio, Examen, Beads, Breath, Daily Office — 25 sessions each), longest-session peaks (Thirty Still / Holy Hour), famous-prayer volume and distinct-count, Names-of-God distinct-count, answered-prayer count (1 / 10 / 50 / 100), testimony-on-answered, partial-answer, voice/journal session counts, Sunday (Sabbath) rhythm, gratitude streaks and volume including a 100-day peak badge, group participation, comeback, time-of-day (early bird / night owl), and leveling / XP milestones up through Level 100.
- Grace Coins awarded by quests and achievements (cosmetic store [POST-MVP]).
- Gratitude logging, Examen completion, and praying for group items all feed the same gamification hot path used by prayer sessions.

3.7 Prayer Groups — Firebase-Backed Shared Intercession [NEEDS IMPLEMENTING]
Purpose: Enable users to create or join strictly invite-only Prayer Groups so they can pray together with friends, family, small groups, or church communities.

**MVP scope decision**: Unlike the solo experience, Prayer Groups require shared state across devices. They are built on **Firebase (Firestore + Google Sign-In via Firebase Auth + Cloud Functions + FCM)** from day one. The project is already Firebase-initialized (`firebase.json`, `functions/`), so we build on that foundation. The rest of the app remains 100% offline-first — Firebase is touched only when a signed-in user opens a Group screen or receives a Group push notification.

**Groups are strictly optional.** A user can use every solo feature of PrayerQuest — prayer modes, gamification, gratitude, answered prayers, fasting, library, crisis mode — without ever signing in. The sign-in prompt appears only when the user taps into the Prayer Groups tab (or the Prayer Partners entry) and chooses to create or join a group. If they never do, they never sign in, and Firebase is never contacted from their device.

Note: A separate step-by-step implementation guide will be provided when Phase 4 begins. This section defines *what* Groups do; the guide defines *how* to wire them up.

3.7.1 Core Features
- **Google Sign-In (via Firebase Auth)** is required on first Group interaction. Users sign in with an existing Google account — no separate email/password account creation. Sign-in is deferred: it is surfaced the first time the user opens the Groups tab, not during onboarding. A signed-out user sees a friendly "Sign in with Google to create or join a Prayer Group" card in place of the Groups list.
- Once signed in, the app pulls the user's groups and group prayer items from Firestore and mirrors them into Room for fast UI. Subsequent actions (creating a group, adding a request, tapping "I prayed for this", updating status) are written to Firestore; the Room mirror is updated by the snapshot listener when Firestore confirms.
- The user can sign out at any time from Settings; signing out detaches all Firestore listeners and hides Groups/Partners UI until they sign back in. Solo features continue to work exactly as before.
- Users can create a new Prayer Group with a name, description, emoji/icon.
- Each group generates a unique **shareable invite code** (e.g., "PRAY-8K9M2X" — 5-byte base32). Only people who receive and enter this code can join.
- No public browsing or discovery.
- Group members view a shared active prayer list, synced via Firestore's offline-capable real-time listeners.
- When any member adds a new prayer request, all members receive an FCM push notification ("Sarah added a new request: 'Healing for Mom'"), respecting the recipient's Quiet Hours.
- Members can mark "I prayed for this" with one tap, or launch any prayer mode against the group item. Count displays ("Prayed by 4 members").
- Optional activity feed showing recent prayer activity (who prayed and when — never revealing private journal content).
- Each group item supports the same update flow as personal prayers: partial answer, modification, or move to Answered.
- **Crucially: there is no "send a message" or "post a comment" feature.** Groups are for shared prayer lists, not chat. The only "communication" is implicit: a notification fires when someone adds a request or marks an item as prayed. This is an intentional anti-social-media design choice.

3.7.2 Privacy & Safety
- Strictly private and invite-only.
- Members can leave a group at any time.
- Group creators (Admin role) can remove members and moderate requests.
- Users sign in with Google, so Firebase receives the Google account's email and display name for authentication purposes. **Inside a group**, other members never see the user's email address; each member is identified only by the per-group display name they chose when joining. Email stays between the user, Google, and Firebase Auth.
- Sign-in is entirely optional (see §3.7.1). Users who never open Groups or Partners never transmit any identity information to Firebase.
- An in-app **Delete Account** flow (Settings → Account) removes the user's Firestore data, revokes Google sign-in, and deletes the Firebase Auth account. A safety-net Cloud Function (`onUserDeleted`) performs the Firestore cleanup on the backend even if the app call is interrupted.
- Firestore Security Rules enforce: only group members can read/write that group's documents.

3.7.3 Real-Time Sync and Offline Behavior
- **Pull path**: when a signed-in user opens the Groups tab or a group detail screen, PrayerGroupRepository opens Firestore snapshot listeners on that group (and on the `userGroups/{uid}/groups` index). Listeners fire on every remote change and update the local Room mirror in real time.
- **Push path**: every user action (create group, join via invite code, add request, tap "I prayed for this", update status, promote/remove member, leave group) writes directly to Firestore; the snapshot listener confirms by updating the Room mirror.
- Listener lifecycle: listeners are attached when a group screen is visible (or the user's groups list is visible) and detached when the screen is disposed. No persistent background listeners — push notifications (FCM) are the wake-up path when the app is closed.
- Firestore's built-in offline cache handles airplane-mode for signed-in users: they can open Groups, view cached prayer items, tap "I prayed for this" (queued), and writes sync when network returns. No manual "refresh" button or action is needed.
- Room mirror is eventually-consistent with Firestore and is used only for fast UI / offline reads. Firestore is the source of truth for all Group data.
- **Signed-out users** see no Group data and initiate no Firestore traffic. The Groups and Partners tabs become simple sign-in prompts.

3.7.4 Gamification Integration
- Praying for group items earns bonus XP and contributes to daily quests.
- Badges: Group Prayer Warrior (first group joined), Faithful Intercessor (50 group requests prayed), Community Builder (create 5 groups), United in Prayer (total group prayers logged).

3.7.5 Technical Notes — Firestore Data Model (sketch)
- `/groups/{groupId}`: name, description, emoji, inviteCode, createdBy (UID), createdAt, memberCount.
- `/groups/{groupId}/members/{uid}`: displayName, role (Admin/Member), joinedAt.
- `/groups/{groupId}/items/{itemId}`: title, description, addedBy, addedAt, status, prayedByCount.
- `/groups/{groupId}/items/{itemId}/prayers/{prayerId}`: memberUid, prayedAt (used for the activity feed and count).
- `/inviteCodes/{code}`: groupId, expiresAt (optional). Cloud Function generates and validates these.
- Room mirror: local Room entities (PrayerGroup, PrayerGroupMember, GroupPrayerItem, GroupPrayerItemCrossRef) cache the user's groups for fast UI. Firestore is the source of truth.

3.13 Notifications & Reminders [NEEDS IMPLEMENTING]
Rich, configurable, considerate.

- **Daily Prayer Reminders**: up to three daily reminders (morning / midday / evening by default). Each reminder has a configurable **time** and a **personality** (short copy variants: "Morning — start the day with Him," "Midday — one breath prayer," "Evening — Examen and gratitude"). Personalities are pre-authored; user can cycle through 3–4 per slot.
- **Streak Alert**: fires late afternoon on a day the user hasn't logged yet — gentle tone, no guilt.
- **Quest Notification**: fires when a daily quest is nearly complete ("One more session and you complete today's quests!").
- **Daily Gratitude Prompt**: separate reminder, configurable time.
- **Prayer Group Activity**: FCM push from Firebase when a member adds a new request (respecting recipient's quiet hours).
- **Quiet Hours**: global setting (default 10 p.m. – 7 a.m.). No notifications of any kind during quiet hours. Violations of quiet hours are a P0 bug.

All local notifications go through WorkManager (reusing ScriptureQuest's worker scaffolding). FCM notifications (Groups only) route through a small Cloud Function.

3.16 Technical Requirements [NEEDS IMPLEMENTING]
- **100% offline-first for all solo features** (Room + WorkManager): prayer engine, gamification, gratitude, answered prayers, fasting, library, crisis mode, onboarding. No account, no network, no sign-in.
- **Firebase (Firestore + Google Sign-In via Firebase Auth + Cloud Functions + FCM) for Prayer Groups and Prayer Partners only.** These features are optional and gated behind Google Sign-In, which is surfaced only at the moment a user tries to use Groups/Partners. Signed-in users pull/push group data against Firestore; signed-out users see a sign-in prompt where the Groups/Partners UI would be and are otherwise unaffected.
- Same architecture and DI as ScriptureQuest (manual AppContainer, companion Factory pattern).
- Material 3 warm faith palette, dark/light mode, premium ad-free tier.
- Voice-to-text everywhere via SpeechRecognizer.
- Photo handling: camera/gallery picker with compression, app-private storage.
- Accessibility: content descriptions, proper contrast, screen reader support, large-text mode.

9. FUTURE UPDATES [FUTURE UPDATE]
Items explicitly deferred from MVP, captured here with enough detail to pick up later without rediscovery.

9.1 Scripture Soak Mode
A Bible-integrated prayer mode. Pulls a verse (from linked ScriptureQuest collection, or from PrayerQuest's bundled prayer-verse pack as fallback). User prays the verse aloud (voice-to-text) or types a personalized prayer over it. Timer + "pray this again tomorrow" option. Was originally MVP Mode #6; moved to FU for launch focus.

9.2 Contemplative Silence Mode
A listening/silence mode. Timer (default 10 minutes) with optional gentle haptic tick every 30–60 seconds. Curated "still small voice" prompts (30–40 handpicked short Scripture passages like "Be still and know that I am God" — Psalm 46:10) surface randomly every 60–90 seconds as a 2-second card. Ends with a quick voice-to-text reflection. No ambient audio (that was sent to §9.5).

9.3 Home-Screen and Lock-Screen Widgets
Home widget variants: streak flame, "tap to log gratitude," daily verse, quick "Start Prayer" button. Lock-screen prayer widget for Android 12+. Widgets are the single biggest retention lever Duolingo invested in late; worth building soon after launch.

9.4 Wear OS Companion
One-tap prayer logging from the wrist. Breath-prayer nudge haptics. Streak status glance. Voice-recording of quick prayers.

9.5 Sacred Soundscapes
Ambient audio library: Gregorian chant, monastery bells, rain, forest, white noise, Celtic harp. Available as a background layer during Contemplative Silence, Voice Record, Lectio Divina, and Breath Prayer modes. Royalty-free or commissioned audio; bundled or downloadable pack.

9.6 Hymn / Worship Lyric Library
Public-domain hymns (Amazing Grace, Be Thou My Vision, Holy Holy Holy, It Is Well, etc.) with full lyrics and a "Pray through this hymn" mode that surfaces stanzas one at a time with pause-and-reflect prompts.

9.7 "Pray Without Ceasing" Bell
Configurable hourly or random gentle chime during waking hours, presenting a 10-second breath prayer card. Opt-in only, very tunable.

9.8 Implementation-Intention Triggers
User configures habit-stacked prayer triggers: "When I sit in my car, pray for my commute." "When I brush my teeth, pray for my spouse." Reminder format is location-based (opt-in) or time-based or contextual.

9.9 Prayer Walk / GPS
Walk-based prayer mode. User chooses a route or starts walking; the app prompts for different prayer focuses at intervals ("Pray for the first person you see," "Pray for your neighborhood," "Pray for the country"). Step counter integration.

9.10 Missionary / Unreached People Group Adoption
Long-term commitment mode where users "adopt" a country, missionary, or unreached people group for a season. Daily reminders, facts, and prayer prompts draw from Operation World / Joshua Project-style data (licensing TBD). Dedicated badges for months-long commitments.

9.11 World/Missions Map Mode
Tap a country on a world map to pray for it, with optional facts (population, Christian percentage, persecution level). Feeds into §9.10.

9.12 Family Mode
Multi-user household prayer app: shared family prayer altar, kid-safe simpler UI for ages 5–12, parent-approved prayer items, shared gratitude wall within the family. Requires account-based multi-user support.

9.13 Prayer Chains
Time-sensitive relay prayer: "Sarah started a prayer chain for Grandpa — pray for 15 minutes, then pass the baton to Mike." Members get a "your turn" notification. Used sparingly for crisis situations in Groups.

9.14 Sermon Notes
Dedicated note-taking UI during church services with offline capture, and a "Pray about this" button that flows each note into the Prayer Items flow. Optional audio recording of the sermon for later reflection.

9.15 Mood / Emotional State Tracker
Log mood before and after each prayer session ("entered: anxious, left: peaceful"). Builds rich personal data over time. Recommends modes based on current emotional state (e.g., anxious → Breath Prayer, grieving → Psalms in Crisis Mode, distracted → Flash-Pray Swipe). Fully on-device and private.

9.16 Spiritual Disciplines Tracker
Beyond prayer: track Bible reading minutes, fasting days, solitude/Sabbath time, acts of service logged, generosity/giving logged, silence/retreat days. Positions PrayerQuest as the whole-formation hub, not just a prayer-habit tool. Each discipline has its own streak, goals, and badges.

9.17 Pastor / Small Group Leader Mode (Premium)
Privacy-first pastoral prayer tracking. Pastors log prayer requests from congregants using tags-only (no names) with custom categories ("marriage," "bereavement," "illness," "doubting," "new believer") and pray through them as a dedicated list. Includes pastoral reflection prompts and a weekly "prayed for my flock" summary.

9.18 Reflective Questions During Contemplation
After Contemplative Silence or Lectio Divina, surface thoughtful reflective prompts: "What word from God came to mind?" "Was there a phrase that stood out?" "What emotion surfaced?" Voice-to-text or text response saved to a Reflection Journal.

9.19 AI-Powered Prayer Suggestions
Free-type a concern ("My dad just got diagnosed with cancer") and get a suggested Scripture, a sample prayer, and a recommended mode. Optional Reflective Companion: gentle AI prompts during Contemplative Silence. Prefer on-device model for privacy where feasible. Premium tier. Must be tastefully gated behind user opt-in with a clear disclosure.

9.20 "Year of Prayer" Share Card
Beautiful shareable image generated from the Year in Review data, designed for Instagram / WhatsApp story sharing. Complementary to the in-app Year in Review experience (which *is* MVP per §3.12.1).

9.21 Cosmetic Themes
Stained glass, garden, minimalist white, dawn/dusk variants. Unlocked with Grace Coins earned from gamification. Cosmetic store.

9.22 Encrypted Google Drive Backup
Encrypted backup of journals, gratitude entries, photos, and answered-prayer testimonies to the user's own Google Drive. Important for long-term testimony preservation across device changes, but out of MVP scope.

9.23 Printable Testimony Books (Print-on-Demand)
Extension of the MVP-scope Testimony Book PDF export (§3.12.3). Optional paid integration with a print-on-demand service to produce a hardcover keepsake book from the user's Answered Prayers.

9.24 Full Cloud Sync for All Solo Features
Optional cloud backup/sync of the entire user state (journals, gratitudes, prayer items, answered prayers, stats) across devices. MVP keeps solo features 100% offline; this is a future convenience feature.

9.25 Group Voice Prayer Rooms
Live audio rooms where group members can pray aloud together in real time. WebRTC-based. Significant moderation and safety design work required before launch.

9.26 AR Prayer Palace
Method-of-loci visual prayer builder using AR. User places prayer items in a virtual 3D space and "walks through" their prayer list. Experimental/speculative.

9.27 Sing / Declare Mode with User Recordings
Users record themselves singing / declaring Scripture or a prayer, and the recordings become a personal worship library for playback during prayer time.

9.28 AI-Suggested Prayer Plans Based on Journal/Mood
Builds on §9.15 and §9.19. AI analyzes journal entries and mood trends to suggest seasonal prayer plans ("It looks like anxiety has been a theme this month — consider a 7-day Philippians 4 plan").
