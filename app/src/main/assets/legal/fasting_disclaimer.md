# Fasting — Important Safety Information

*Please read this carefully before starting any fast longer than 24 hours.*

---

## The core disclaimer (from DD §3.11)

> Fasting affects your body. PrayerQuest is not medical advice. If you have a medical condition (including but not limited to diabetes, eating disorder history, pregnancy, heart condition, or are on medication), are underweight, or are under 18, please consult a physician before fasting. Stop immediately and seek medical attention if you feel unwell.

## Not medical, psychological, or nutritional advice

PrayerQuest is a spiritual formation and prayer-habit app. The Fasting Tracker exists so you can pair prayer with an intentional fast and reflect on the experience. It is **not** a medical service, a dietitian, a mental-health provider, or a substitute for any of them. The information in the app — including timers, journal prompts, reminders, badges, and any educational text about fasting traditions — is for general informational and spiritual purposes only, and should never be interpreted as a personalized recommendation to fast, to continue fasting, or to break a fast in any particular way.

If you have any question at all about whether fasting is safe for you, talk to a qualified healthcare professional **before** you begin. We strongly encourage you to do this the first time you fast, whenever you change the type or length of your fast, and any time your health situation changes.

## Do not begin a fast without medical guidance if any of the following apply to you

- You are pregnant, trying to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.
- You are under 18 years old.
- You have (or have ever had) an eating disorder, disordered eating patterns, or a complicated relationship with food, body image, or weight.
- You are underweight or have recently lost weight unintentionally.
- You have diabetes (Type 1 or Type 2), hypoglycemia, or any condition that affects blood sugar regulation.
- You have a heart condition, blood-pressure issues, kidney or liver disease, or a history of stroke.
- You take any prescription medication — including but not limited to insulin, blood-pressure medication, blood thinners, lithium, antidepressants, antipsychotics, or medication that requires food for proper absorption.
- You are recovering from surgery, illness, or injury.
- You are elderly or frail.
- Your doctor, therapist, or dietitian has previously advised you not to fast.

This list is not exhaustive. When in doubt, talk to a professional.

## Stop the fast immediately if

- You feel faint, dizzy, or lightheaded.
- You experience chest pain, an irregular heartbeat, or shortness of breath.
- You experience severe headache, confusion, blurred vision, or slurred speech.
- You vomit, have persistent nausea, or feel unable to keep water down.
- You feel unusually cold, shaky, or cannot stop trembling.
- You notice any symptoms of low blood sugar (sweating, weakness, irritability, confusion).
- You feel mentally unwell in a way that concerns you — including thoughts about your body, food, or self-worth that feel distressing.
- You feel worse, not better, as the fast continues.

**If you feel unwell, stop immediately and seek medical attention.** If symptoms are severe — chest pain, trouble breathing, loss of consciousness, or any symptom that feels like an emergency — call your local emergency number or go to the nearest emergency room. Your health matters more than any spiritual practice or app streak.

## Fasting is not a weight-loss program

PrayerQuest's Fasting Tracker is designed to pair fasting with prayer as a historic Christian discipline. It is not a weight-loss tool, a diet plan, or a metabolic-intervention protocol. Please do not use it for those purposes. If you are struggling with food, body image, or weight in a way that is distressing you, we gently encourage you to talk to a qualified mental-health professional or a registered dietitian.

## Spiritual note

A fast is an offering, not a performance. If your health or circumstances mean you cannot fast from food, there are many other faithful ways to draw near to God — a media fast, a fast from a specific comfort, extended prayer, acts of service, or simply a focused season of Scripture reading. God receives a willing heart; He does not require you to harm yourself to be close to Him.
